You're staying right in the heart of the action, by Jiefangbei, the Liberation Monument and the city's main downtown pedestrian core on the Yuzhong peninsula. This is the dense, vertical, neon Chongqing of the postcards: Hongyadong, the Yangtze cable car, and the riverfront are all within walking distance or a short hop. Being central means it's lively and a little loud, and in Chongqing 'walkable' can mean stairs and steep lanes between street levels, but you couldn't be better placed for the city's signature sights, most of them lit up beautifully after dark.

FRI JUL 3 — Wartime Capital

8 stops
Phrase of the day微辣wēi là“way lah”mildly spicy — the wise hotpot order

You arrive in Chongqing by train at midday and the city announces itself immediately, there's no easing in to a place built vertically across river gorges. After checking into the Maiyue Hotel by Jiefangbei, head straight to Renmin Square: the Great Hall of the People and the free Three Gorges Museum are directly across the plaza from each other and together make the best cultural morning in the city, Ba-Shu bronzes, WWII bombing raids, and the artifacts pulled from the gorges before the dam rose. The Planning Exhibition Hall nearby puts Chongqing's impossible topography into a scale model you can actually read. The Lianglukou Grand Escalator on the way back is ten minutes and worth every second. By late afternoon, make for Ciqikou, the old riverside town, and as dusk falls the day ends at its two signature experiences: dinner at the Filling Station underground hotpot and Hongyadong lit up over the Jialing River after dark. Show your allergy card at the Filling Station and ask about the oil; peanut oil is a real risk in Chongqing hotpot.

2

Great Hall of the People 重庆人民大礼堂

Landmark
Hours: Exterior daily; interior ~08:30–17:30Suggested time: 30–45 minFrom previous: ~15 min metro from hotel (Jiefangbei to Renmin Square)Prior booking?: No
Address: No. 173 Renmin Rd, Yuzhong District
A Soviet-era take on Chinese palace architecture, finished in 1954 and modeled loosely on the Temple of Heaven. The green-tiled dome and sweeping staircase make it the grandest civic building in western China. You're here for the exterior; the auditorium inside only opens for events.
Pro Tips Photograph it from the plaza, then cross to the Three Gorges Museum. The Three Gorges Antique Market nearby is worth a browse for curios and Mao-era memorabilia if time allows.
"'Impressive outside, skip the inside.' Netizens say interior is dated, not worth ¥10-20. Best viewed across the plaza from Three Gorges Museum. Free exterior. Quick photo stop."
3

Three Gorges Museum 重庆中国三峡博物馆

Museum
Hours: Tue–Sun 09:00–17:00 (last entry 16:30)Suggested time: 2–3 hrFrom previous: ~3 min walk (across plaza)Prior booking?: No*
Address: No. 236 Renmin Rd, Yuzhong District
The major museum of the region, with 170,000-plus artifacts. The collections to prioritize: Ba-Shu bronzes from the cultures that predate Chinese rule here, artifacts salvaged before the Three Gorges Dam flooded a thousand archaeological sites, and the galleries on the WWII bombing of Chongqing, when this was China's wartime capital under siege.
Pro Tips Free with passport registration. Give it 2 to 3 hours and don't skip the wartime galleries upstairs. Directly across the plaza from the Great Hall, so the pairing is automatic.
"'Excellent and underrated.' Highlights: Ba-Shu bronzes, dam-rescue relics, WWII bombing history. Free but WeChat reservation. Allow 2-3 hrs. Closed Mon. Combine with Great Hall."
4

Lianglukou Grand Escalator 两路口大手扫梯

Transit
Hours: 06:30–23:00Suggested time: 15 minFrom previous: ~10 min metro (Lines 1/3 from Renmin Square)Prior booking?: No
Address: Lianglukou, Yuzhong District
A 367-foot escalator climbing 174 feet at a 30-degree pitch, the third-highest in Asia. Only a Pyongyang Metro escalator (about 210 feet of rise) and the Rustaveli station escalator in Tbilisi (197 feet) beat it. Built in 1996 to replace a funicular, it hauls commuters between the Lianglukou metro interchange and the old Caiyuanba railway station below. Pure infrastructure that happens to be spectacular: the ride takes 2.5 minutes and costs ¥2.
Pro Tips Ride it downhill from Lianglukou station (Exit 3, signs point the way) for the full vertigo. It's the same fare system as the metro. Eling Park is nearby if you want panoramic views afterward. Fun footnote for the infrastructure-minded: Chongqing municipality also now holds the world record for longest outdoor escalator system, the 905-meter Wushan Goddess escalator that opened in February 2026, three hours east of the city.
"'A commuter escalator that feels like an amusement ride.' A fun way to feel Chongqing's verticality. ¥2 (metro fare). Hours follow metro schedule. Ride once for the novelty."
5

Chongqing Planning Exhibition Hall 重庆规划展览馆

Museum
Hours: 09:00–17:00, last 16:30 (closed Mon)Suggested time: 1–1.5 hrFrom previous: ~8 min walk (both on Renmin Square)Prior booking?: No
Address: Near People's Great Hall, Yuzhong District
Chongqing's answer to the planning halls you saw in Chengdu, and the best single primer on why this city looks the way it does. The centerpiece is a giant scale model of the municipality that makes the vertical geography click: ridgelines, two rivers, buildings stacked up cliff faces. Interactive exhibits cover the megaprojects in progress.
Pro Tips Head straight for the big model and spend your time there. It's near the Great Hall and Three Gorges Museum, so all three combine into one walking loop. Bring your passport for registration.
"'Best way to understand Chongqing's 3D madness.' Netizens love the giant scale model showing vertical topography. Free with passport. Combine with People's Great Hall + Three Gorges Museum. Confirm location on Amap."
6

Ciqikou Ancient Town 磁器口古镇

Landmark
Hours: 09:00–22:00Suggested time: 1–2 hrFrom previous: ~20 min metro (Line 1, Renmin Square to Ciqikou)Prior booking?: No
Address: Shapingba District
A 1,700-year-old riverside village swallowed by the city, with Ming and Qing era lanes that are now firmly on the tourist circuit. Crowded, yes. The real reason to come is the Chongqing 1949 Show staged nearby, a rotating-theater production about the city at the end of the civil war that locals rate as the best big show in town. The lanes themselves are good for an hour of snacks and browsing.
Pro Tips Book 1949 Show tickets well ahead; it sells out. In the lanes, try mao xue wang and the thousand-layer sesame cakes. Metro Line 1 runs straight to Ciqikou station. Weekday afternoons are far calmer.
"'Touristy but fun.' Main draw is the 1949 show, not the town itself. Food stalls use nuts - allergy cards. Free. Show sells out - book on Damai. Crowded weekends."
7

Filling Station (地下之城 Underground Hotpot) 地下之城老火锅

Food
Hours: ~11:00–22:00 (confirm in advance)Suggested time: 1.5–2 hrFrom previous: ~10 min walk from CiqikouPrior booking?: Rec.
Address: Yuzhong District cliff face, near Ciqikou/Hongyadong, Chongqing
Hotpot inside the wartime air raid shelters carved into the Yuzhong cliffs. During the bombing campaign of 1938 to 1943 these tunnels held thousands of sheltering residents; now one section serves spicy beef-tallow hotpot under bare rock. Dinner here is half meal, half history lesson, and fully Chongqing.
Pro Tips Reserve ahead, especially weekends. Order the classic spicy broth (or a split pot if you want an exit ramp from the heat). The tunnels run cool, so bring a layer. The entrance is easy to walk past; follow the cliff face.
"'Underground hotpot in the bomb shelters - surreal and delicious.' Netizens say it's theatrical + genuinely good. Book ahead. Cold in the tunnels. ALLERGY: ask specifically about peanut oil in the broth + sesame in dipping sauces."
8

Hongyadong Folk Custom Scene Area 洪崖洞

Landmark
Hours: 10:00–22:00 (best at night)Suggested time: 1–1.5 hrFrom previous: ~10 min walk from Filling StationPrior booking?: No
Address: Cangbai Rd, Yuzhong District 400011
⚠️ UPDATED 2026: Free entry but requires advance reservation via the official Hongyadong WeChat mini-program. Lights switch on at 19:30. An 11-story stilted folk-village complex hanging off the cliff over the Jialing River, and the single most photographed thing in the city. Inside it's shops and restaurants; outside, after dark, it glows like the bathhouse from Spirited Away.
Pro Tips Reserve on the WeChat mini-program before you go, then arrive 30 minutes before the 19:30 light-up to stake out a position. The classic full-facade shot is from Qianximen Bridge across the river. Skip the interior shops; the building is the show.
"'Spirited Away in real life', magical at night. Best photo: Qianximen Bridge. Go after 8 PM. Free. Walkable from hotel."

SAT JUL 4 — 3D City

6 stops
Phrase of the day好吃hǎochī“how-chrr”delicious

A focused afternoon-and-evening in the Jiefangbei and river-crossing parts of the city. Start at the Chongqing Art Gallery and work through the two Jiefangbei shopping and viewpoint stops nearby. The evening crosses the river: Firefly Bay's Maomao theme park on the south bank, the Yangtze Ropeway strung across the gorge, and the drone show over the river confluence at Chaotianmen to close the night. A lighter day after Friday's museum run, but the ropeway and the drone show are the two things that make Chongqing feel like no other city on the trip.

Yangtze River Ropeway (cableway) 长江索道

Activity
Hours: 08:00–21:00 (last entry 21:00)Suggested time: 30–45 min (4-min ride)From previous: ~6 min walk from prevPrior booking?: Rec.
Address: No. 151 Xinhua Rd, Yuzhong District (north stn)
The cable car across the Yangtze, once daily transit for river-crossing commuters, now the most popular way to see the city from the air. Four minutes gliding over the river with the skyline, gorge, and stacked hillside neighborhoods below.
Pro Tips Buy tickets ahead on Trip.com or you'll queue, sometimes for over an hour on weekends. Ride toward the south bank for the better skyline angle, and morning light is best for photos. The north terminal is walkable from Jiefangbei.
"'Iconic but crowded.' Book online + board from quieter station. Best at dusk. 4-min ride, camera ready. Queue hits 1 hr; pre-booking helps."
6

Drone Show at Chaotianmen River 朝天门无人机表演

Activity
Hours: 20:45:00Suggested time: 45–60 minFrom previous: ~5 min walk from cableway north stationPrior booking?: No
Address: Chaotianmen Riverfront, Yuzhong District, Chongqing
A large-scale drone light show over the confluence of the Yangtze and Jialing rivers at Chaotianmen, the tip of the peninsula. Hundreds of drones forming images over the water with the Raffles City horizontal-skyscraper complex behind. When it runs, it's a stunner.
Pro Tips Not nightly. Confirm the schedule on Dianping or Xiaohongshu before building the evening around it. Best vantage points are the Chaotianmen docks or across on the Jiangbei side.
"Netizens call it 'breathtaking' when it runs. Not a daily show, confirm schedule on Dianping or Xiaohongshu before building your evening around it."

Checkout morning. Leave the hotel by 8:30 AM for the 11:05 AM flight to Sanya. Jiefangbei square is two minutes from the lobby, worth a last look before loading out. Jiefangbei (People's Liberation Monument) 解放碑

Landmark
Hours: 24 hr (shops ~10:00–22:00)Suggested time: 15 minFrom previous: ~5 min walkPrior booking?: No
Address: Zourong Rd, Yuzhong District 400010
The 27-meter clock tower at the center of downtown, built in 1947 to mark victory over Japan — the only WWII victory monument of its kind in China. The pedestrian district around it is the commercial heart of the city. The hotel is two minutes away; a quick circuit around the base on departure morning makes a good final Chongqing moment.
Pro Tips You'll walk past this every day in Jiefangbei. On departure morning, a five-minute detour to the base before loading out is worth it for a final photo with the tower lit in the early light.
"'The heart of Chongqing' - best at night when lit. Free. Ringed by luxury malls. Three Gorges Antique Market nearby worth a browse. Quick photo stop."
2

Chongqing East Railway Station (optional) 重庆东站

Train Station
Hours: 24 hr (shops 07:00–22:00)Suggested time: 30–60 minFrom previous: ~35 min metro from Jiefangbei (opposite direction from airport)Prior booking?: No
Address: Nan'an/Banan area (2024 mega-hub)
The world's largest railway station by floor area: 1.22 million sq m, 15 platforms, 29 tracks. A staggering piece of infrastructure worth 20 minutes if you have them. Timing caveat: it's in the opposite direction from CKG airport. From here to the airport is roughly 45-55 min — only realistic on Sunday if you head there by 7:00 AM and keep it to a quick walk through the main hall.
Pro Tips Treat this as a drive-by, not a stop. A taxi from Jiefangbei to the station and straight on to the airport may work better than two metro rides on departure morning.
"'World's largest station - a megastructure spectacle.' Opened 2024, 15 million sq ft. Infrastructure fans only; you don't depart here. Free to view. Out of the way - only with spare time."

Read before Chongqing

Through the eras

1890s

Forced open as a treaty port in 1890, the furthest inland the Western powers pried China open, bringing foreign trade and steamships hundreds of miles up the Yangtze into the mountainous interior.

WWII (1938–45)

Chongqing's defining hour: when Japan seized the coast, it became China's wartime capital (1938–45). Shielded by mountains and notorious fog but bombed relentlessly from the air for years, its people sheltered in tunnels blasted into the cliffs, some of which are now the hot-pot caverns and shops you'll visit. The Three Gorges Museum tells this story powerfully.

Today

Made a directly-governed municipality in 1997, Chongqing exploded into one of the world's largest urban areas (30+ million in its administrative region) and the booming gateway to China's developing west. Its surreal vertical cityscape, metros through buildings, stacked highways, cliffside towers, has made it a viral 'cyberpunk' destination.

✓Understand Chongqing — the 3D city▾

Most cities are read on a flat map. Chongqing has to be read in cross-section. Built on a red-sandstone promontory where the Jialing River meets the Yangtze, the old core is effectively a peninsula clinging to hillsides, and the terrain, not any planner, dictated how the city grew. Roads stack on roads; a building's 'ground floor' might be its tenth; metro lines burst out of cliffs and run through apartment blocks. Locals call it the '8D city,' and the joke is that your navigation app simply gives up. The municipality is enormous and mostly mountainous, over 30 million people across an area the size of a small country, but as a visitor you'll spend nearly all your time in Yuzhong, the central peninsula district that holds Jiefangbei, Hongyadong, and the riverfronts. Across the water, Nan'an offers the One Tree lookout and the south-bank views; Shapingba holds Ciqikou old town. The rivers are the organizing logic of everything: where they meet (Chaotianmen), what crosses them (the cable car, dozens of bridges), and how the city climbs away from them. Administratively, Chongqing is one of only four cities that report directly to Beijing rather than to a province, but culturally it's still Sichuanese at heart, which is to say: built around the hotpot table, and unbothered by your spice tolerance.

✓The Mountain City (山城): vertical urbanism▾

Chongqing is built on mountains where the Yangtze and Jialing rivers meet, so the city goes vertical in ways nowhere else does: monorails punch through apartment buildings, a single tower can have its ground floor on level 1 and *also* on level 22, and escalators and cable cars work as everyday commuting. Locals call it the 'Mountain City' (山城). The disorientation is the point, give in to it and just ride the metro and the ropeway for the views.

山城 shān chéng'Mountain City' — Chongqing's nickname for its vertical, hill-stacked cityscape.

✓Mountain city, river city▾

Chongqing's whole culture is shaped by its impossible terrain, built vertically across the steep confluence of the Yangtze and Jialing rivers. For most of its history the rivers were the only highways, making it the great trans-shipment port of the upper Yangtze, where the goods of the southwest met the river trade downstream to Shanghai. The cable cars, cliffside stilt houses, monorail through a building, and the boatmen's culture all flow from a city that learned to live on near-vertical riverbanks.

✓Hot pot origins▾

Chongqing hot pot was born on these riverbanks among boat trackers and dockworkers, who cooked cheap offcuts in a cauldron of chili, Sichuan peppercorn and beef tallow to ward off the river damp. The result is málà. 'numbing-spicy', the tingling, fiery flavor that defines the city. You cook raw ingredients yourself in the bubbling broth. Order a split pot so you can dial the heat, and remember refined oils are used here, confirm with our allergen cards regardless.

麻辣 má là'Numbing-spicy' — the tongue-tingling Sichuan peppercorn + chili sensation.

✓Hotpot: the boatmen's meal▾

Chongqing hotpot was born on the Yangtze docks. The story goes that boat haulers and dockworkers cooked cheap offcuts, tripe, offal, in a fiery communal broth of beef tallow, chilies, and Sichuan peppercorns to ward off the river damp. What began as poor men's food became the city's defining ritual and its most famous export. The numbing, oily, intensely spicy Chongqing style is distinct from milder hotpots elsewhere, and a real allergy minefield, since peanut and sesame turn up in broths and dipping sauces.

✓The Three Gorges & the river▾

Chongqing sits at the head of the Three Gorges, the dramatic canyons where the Yangtze cuts through the mountains downstream. The colossal Three Gorges Dam (completed 2012, the world's largest power station) raised the river and submerged towns and antiquities, many rescued artifacts now live in the city's Three Gorges Museum. The river is Chongqing's reason for being: trade artery, defensive moat, and the source of its fog and its food.

✓The Ba people and an ancient identity▾

Long before it was Chinese in any modern sense, this was the land of the Ba, an ancient kingdom absorbed by the Qin in the 4th century BC, leaving a distinct regional identity, bronze artifacts, and folklore that the Three Gorges Museum documents. The rugged, fiercely independent Ba reputation still feeds Chongqing's self-image as a tough, spicy, do-it-its-own-way city, culturally its own thing, even though it was administratively part of Sichuan until 1997.

✓A treaty port opened to the world (1890s)▾

Chongqing's modern history opens in 1890–91, when it became a treaty port, the furthest-inland point opened to foreign trade on the Yangtze. British and later Japanese concessions established themselves on the river, steamships eventually conquered the treacherous Three Gorges passage, and this remote mountain city was wired into global commerce. The opening seeded the foreign banks, trading houses, and consulates whose traces still mark the older riverfront districts.

✓A city that has governed itself twice over▾

Chongqing holds an unusual place in modern China, it has been a directly-governed seat of power not once but twice. When Japan took the coast in 1937, the Nationalist government fled up the Yangtze and made Chongqing the provisional wartime capital; for eight years, government ministries, universities, and entire factories relocated here and the population more than tripled under relentless bombing. Then in 1997 it was split from Sichuan and made a province-level municipality answering directly to Beijing, one of only four (with Beijing, Shanghai, Tianjin). Its boundaries swell to a province-sized 82,000 km² and over 30 million people, though most of that is rural; the actual city is around 16 million. It governs itself like a province because, in scale, it is one.

✓WWII wartime capital▾

During WWII, after Japan took the coast, Chongqing became China's wartime capital (1938–45). Shielded by mountains and fog, it endured years of relentless aerial bombing, among the most-bombed cities of the war, while sheltering its population in tunnels carved into the rock. Some of those air-raid shelters are now restaurants and shops; the hot pot cavern on the itinerary is literally one of them. The Three Gorges Museum tells this story powerfully.

✓Wartime capital and the bombing (1938–45)▾

From 1938 to 1943, Chongqing endured one of the most sustained aerial bombing campaigns in history, as Japan tried to break Free China's will from the air. The population sheltered in vast tunnel networks carved into the bedrock, the same caves that now house atmospheric hotpot restaurants. As the Nationalist wartime capital, the city absorbed the relocated government, refugees, universities, and entire factories; its population more than tripled, and it became the diplomatic stage where China and the Allies coordinated the Pacific war.
